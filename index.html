Summary: Latitude vs. X

The purpose of this project was to analyze the relationship between weather and the Earth's latitude. Does the Earth's weather changes as you get closer to the equator (0 degrees Latitude)? If so, what are the affects of the weather changes?

To accomplish this analysis, we first pulled data from the OpenWeatherMap.org's API to assemble a dataset on over 500 cities at various latitudes between the North Pole at 90 degrees North (+90 degrees) and the South Pole at 90 degrees South (-90 degrees).

After assembling the dataset, we used Matplotlib to plot various aspects of the weather vs. the cities' latitude. Factors we looked at included: Maximum Temperature, Humidity, Cloudiness, and Windspeed. This site provides the source data and visualizations created as part of the analysis, as well as, explanations and descriptions of any trends and correlations witnessed.
